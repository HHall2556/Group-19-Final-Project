Project Clean Air Contact and Volunteer Opportunities/Tips

Making a conscious effort to change your day to day lifestyle is an awesome thing to do for the planet. No matter how small the change is, we can all pitch in in helping to keep the air unpolluted for generations to come. here are a few ways to reduce your carbon fingerprint or help volunteer

1. Rely less on fossil fuel (driving your car) and use alternatives
An estimated 150 million people living in the U.S. live in areas that do not meet air quality standards, and most of this bad air is due to our own reliance on fossil fuels Combustion engines from cars and trucks produce a number of polluting elements including ozone, particulate matter, and other smog-forming emissions. Nitrogen oxide and carbon monoxide are other main components of this exhaust, and both have had a proven negative impact on the environment. Alternatives to driving could be to walk or bike around. If the distant feels lengthy then try using electric cars or commute by either using the metro or the bus to reach those destinations

2. Funding or raising money to help with projects dealing with the problem like Clean Air Fund
If you could go the extra mile and donate to projects that are helping make breathable air still a thing then for sure go for it. If money seems like an issue then start a fundraiser to get other people's attention on the subject. Bakesales at universities or bazzaars could help getting things running. Clean Air Fund tackles the issue and is backed by organization. Learn more about them here You can also plant trees or get involved with Arbor Day
